## Welcome to **Python Pathways: From Zero to Builder**

Hi! Thank you for trusting **Dense Sparse, Inc.** with seven weeks of your child's summer. I'm **Venkata Chintapalli**, the instructor, and this short page tells you everything you need to know to set your student up for success.

### What is this course?

A 7-week, hands-on Python course (June 16 – August 3, 2026) that takes a child from *zero programming experience* to *something they built themselves and are proud to demo*.

- **Track A (5th grade):** by Week 7, your child will demo a complete playable arcade game (Snake / Space Invaders / Flappy Bird / Tetris) they coded in Python + Pygame.
- **Track B (10th grade):** by Week 7, your student will present a working **patient risk-scoring tool** built with real medical datasets — directly relevant to a future MD or NP path.

### Why it's valuable

Coding teaches careful thinking, debugging (which is just persistent problem-solving), and the joy of *making something real*. We do not teach memorization. We teach building. Every single live session ends with something visible the student can show you that night.

### What I Ask of You as a Parent

1. **Be available, not hovering.** Sit in the next room. Don't sit at the screen with them.
2. **Don't write their code for them.** Even if it's faster. Especially if it's faster.
3. **Celebrate the showy stuff.** When they say "look what I made!" — go look.
4. **Email me if anything is off** — fatigue, frustration, family schedule changes. I'd rather hear early than guess.
5. **For Track A only:** sit in for the first 5 minutes of Session 1 to confirm Replit, Zoom, and the course site all work on their machine.
